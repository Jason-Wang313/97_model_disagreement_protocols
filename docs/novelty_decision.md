# Novelty Decision

Chosen thesis: classify model disagreement and map it to robot action protocols.

Decision after expanded v5 evidence: KILL_ARCHIVE.

Reason: the claimed novelty is not empirically decisive. The v5 audit shows that typed disagreement can reduce false alarms, but the full mechanism loses the deployment objectives to recovery-first and failure-aware baselines, and ablations remove or simplify parts of the mechanism without hurting the measured objective.

Future revival would need external evidence that the type-to-protocol mechanism, not merely recovery/fallback, causes robust robot performance gains under fixed safety budgets.
